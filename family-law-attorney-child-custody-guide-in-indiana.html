Understanding Child Custody in Indiana

Child custody refers to the legal rights and responsibilities of parents or guardians to make decisions for their children. In Indiana, child custody is governed by the Indiana Code, specifically Title 31, Family Law.

Types of Child Custody in Indiana

Indiana law recognizes several types of child custody arrangements:

  • Custodial Parent**: The parent with whom the child resides most of the time.
  • Possessory Custodian**: The parent with visitation rights, but not primary physical custody.
  • Shared Custody**: Both parents share physical and decision-making responsibilities.
  • Joint Custody**: Both parents make decisions jointly, but one parent may have primary physical custody.

Indiana's Best Interests Standard

When determining child custody, Indiana courts apply the "best interests" standard, which considers the following factors:

  • Wishes of the child's parents
  • Wishes of the child, depending on the child's age and maturity
  • Interaction and interrelationship of the child with parents, siblings, and any other person who may significantly affect the child's best interests
  • Child's adjustment to home, school, and community
  • Willingness and ability of each parent to facilitate a close and continuing relationship between the child and the other parent
  • Physical violence or threat of physical violence by the parent or other person toward the child or other parent
  • Contribution of each parent to the child's care and support
  • Home, school, and community record of the child
  • Moral fitness of the parents
  • Physical and mental health of the parents
  • Child's cultural background
  • Any other factors the court finds to be relevant in determining the child's best interests

Common Child Custody Disputes in Indiana

Indiana courts often encounter the following child custody disputes:

Relocation

When a parent seeks to relocate with the child to another state or significant distance, the court may need to determine whether the move is in the child's best interests.

Visitation Rights

Disputes often arise when parents disagree on visitation schedules, including frequency, duration, and specific dates and times.

Custody Modifications

When circumstances change, parents may seek to modify existing custody arrangements, such as when a new partner moves in or a parent's work schedule changes.

Indiana Child Custody Laws and Statutes

Indiana Code, specifically Title 31, Family Law, governs child custody in the state. Key statutes include:

Indiana Code 31-17-2-8: Best Interests Standard

This statute outlines the factors courts consider when determining child custody.

Indiana Code 31-17-2-19: Relocation

This statute governs relocation disputes, requiring parents to provide written notice and demonstrate the relocation is in the child's best interests.

Indiana Code 31-17-2-23: Visitation Rights

This statute sets forth the guidelines for visitation schedules, including minimum contact requirements.

Indiana Child Custody Proceedings

The following is a step-by-step guide to Indiana child custody proceedings:

Initial Petition

The process begins with a parent filing a petition for custody, which may be accompanied by a motion for temporary custody and visitation.

Child Custody Evaluation

Courts may order a child custody evaluation to assess the child's best interests, which may involve interviews with the child, parents, and other relevant parties.

Custody Hearing

The court holds a hearing to determine custody, considering the best interests standard and evidence presented by both parties.

Custody Order

The court issues a custody order, which may include a visitation schedule, decision-making authority, and other relevant provisions.

Indiana Child Custody Attorney Costs

Custody disputes can be costly, with attorney fees ranging from $200 to $500 per hour, depending on the location and experience of the attorney.

Factors Affecting Attorney Costs

  • Complexity of the case
  • Attorney experience and reputation
  • Location (urban vs. rural)
  • Number of court appearances
  • Time spent on discovery, motion practice, and trial preparation

Indiana Child Custody FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about Indiana child custody:

Q: What is the best interests standard in Indiana?

A: The best interests standard in Indiana considers factors such as the wishes of the child's parents, the child's wishes, interaction and interrelationship with parents and other relevant parties, and the child's adjustment to home, school, and community.

Q: Can I relocate with my child to another state or significant distance?

A: Yes, but you must provide written notice and demonstrate the relocation is in the child's best interests.

Q: How long does a custody case take in Indiana?

A: Custody cases in Indiana can take several months to several years to resolve, depending on the complexity of the case and the court's schedule.

Q: Can I appeal a custody decision in Indiana?

A: Yes, but you must file a timely appeal and demonstrate reversible error in the trial court's decision.

Q: What are the costs associated with an Indiana child custody case?

A: Attorney fees can range from $200 to $500 per hour, depending on the location and experience of the attorney.
