COMMON LEGAL PROCEEDINGS

The following are common legal proceedings in Family Law, Divorce, and Child Custody cases in South Carolina:

  • No-Fault Divorce: A divorce action where one spouse files a complaint alleging irreconcilable differences.
  • Equitable Distribution: The division of marital property and debts in a divorce action.
  • Custody and Visitation: Determining the rights and responsibilities of parents regarding their minor children.
  • Child Support: Determining the amount of child support to be paid by one parent to the other.
  • Enforcement of Support Orders: Taking action to enforce a court order requiring one parent to pay child support.
  • Modification of Support Orders: Changing a court order requiring one parent to pay child support.

FAQs

The following are frequently asked questions (FAQs) about Family Law, Divorce, and Child Custody in South Carolina:

  • Q: Can I get a divorce in South Carolina if I'm not a resident?

    • A: Yes, you can get a divorce in South Carolina if you're not a resident, but you must meet the state's residency requirements.

  • Q: How do I get custody of my child in South Carolina?

    • A: To get custody of your child in South Carolina, you must file a complaint with the court and present evidence and arguments in support of your claim.

  • Q: Can I modify a child support order in South Carolina?

    • A: Yes, you can modify a child support order in South Carolina if there's a significant change in circumstances that warrants a change in the support amount.

  • Q: How long does a divorce take in South Carolina?

    • A: The length of a divorce in South Carolina can vary depending on the complexity of the case and the parties' ability to agree on the terms of the divorce.
