Understanding Statute of Limitations in South Carolina

The statute of limitations is a law that sets a time limit for filing a lawsuit or taking legal action in family law cases. In South Carolina, the SOL varies depending on the type of case:

Personal Injury

For personal injury cases, the SOL in South Carolina is 4 years from the date of the injury. This means that if a family member is injured due to another person's negligence or wrongdoing, they have 4 years to file a lawsuit seeking compensation.

Property Damage

In property damage cases, the SOL in South Carolina is 2 years from the date of the damage. This means that if a family member's property is damaged due to another person's negligence or wrongdoing, they have 2 years to file a lawsuit seeking compensation.

Breach of Contract

For breach of contract cases, the SOL in South Carolina is 6 years from the date of the breach. This means that if a family member's contract is breached, they have 6 years to file a lawsuit seeking compensation or enforcement of the contract.
