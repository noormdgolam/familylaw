MICHIGAN FAMILY LAW: DIVORCE, CHILD CUSTODY, AND STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS

As a highly experienced Family Law attorney and legal consultant, we have compiled a comprehensive guide to navigating the complex world of divorce, child custody, and statute of limitations in Michigan. This article will provide an in-depth examination of state-specific statutes, common legal proceedings, expected attorney costs, and step-by-step guidance to ensure that you are well-informed and prepared throughout the process.

Understanding Michigan Family Law

Michigan is an equitable distribution state, which means that marital property will be divided fairly and equitably between spouses, rather than on a 50/50 basis. To initiate a divorce in Michigan, one spouse must file a complaint for divorce with the court, which will then issue a summons requiring the other spouse to respond.

Statute of Limitations in Michigan

The statute of limitations in Michigan refers to the amount of time that a person has to file a lawsuit or take legal action. In the context of divorce and child custody, the statute of limitations is critical, as it determines how long a person has to file for divorce, seek custody of a child, or enforce a court order.

Divorce Statute of Limitations:

  • One year from the date of separation, or
  • Two years from the date of the last cohabitation.

Child Custody Statute of Limitations:

  • Two years from the date of the child's birth, or
  • One year from the date of the child's emancipation.

Enforcing a Court Order:

  • Within one year from the date of the court order, or
  • Within three years from the date of the court order, if the order was entered prior to 1998.

Common Legal Proceedings in Michigan Family Law

Michigan family law courts handle a wide range of cases, including:

Divorce Proceedings:

  • Uncontested divorce: A divorce in which both spouses agree on the terms of the divorce.
  • Contested divorce: A divorce in which the spouses do not agree on the terms of the divorce.
  • Irreconcilable differences divorce: A divorce in which the court finds that the marriage is irretrievably broken due to irreconcilable differences.

Child Custody Proceedings:

  • Custody by agreement: A custody arrangement in which both parents agree on the terms of custody.
  • Custody by court order: A custody arrangement in which the court makes a decision regarding custody.
  • Modification of custody: A change to an existing custody order.

FAQs in Michigan Family Law

Here are some frequently asked questions and answers to help you navigate Michigan family law:

Q: What is the difference between a divorce and a separation?

A: A divorce is a legal termination of a marriage, while a separation is a decision to live apart from one's spouse without formally terminating the marriage.

Q: Can I get a divorce in Michigan without going to court?

A: Yes, you can file for an uncontested divorce in Michigan without going to court, as long as both spouses agree on the terms of the divorce.

Q: Can I modify a custody order in Michigan?

A: Yes, you can modify a custody order in Michigan if there has been a significant change in circumstances since the original order was entered.

Q: How long does a divorce take in Michigan?

A: The length of a divorce in Michigan can vary widely, depending on the complexity of the case and the court's schedule.

Q: Do I need an attorney to get a divorce in Michigan?

A: While it is not required to have an attorney to get a divorce in Michigan, it is highly recommended to ensure that your rights are protected and that your divorce is handled efficiently and effectively.

Q: Can I get child support in Michigan?

A: Yes, you can seek child support in Michigan if you are a parent with a child who is dependent on you for financial support.

Q: Can I get spousal support in Michigan?

A: Yes, you can seek spousal support in Michigan if you are a spouse who needs financial support due to a disability or other circumstances.

Q: Can I get a restraining order in Michigan?

A: Yes, you can seek a restraining order in Michigan if you are experiencing domestic violence or harassment.

Q: How do I file for divorce in Michigan?

A: To file for divorce in Michigan, you will need to file a complaint for divorce with the court, pay the filing fee, and serve the complaint on your spouse.

Q: What is the process for modifying a child support order in Michigan?

A: To modify a child support order in Michigan, you will need to file a motion to modify with the court, pay the filing fee, and attend a hearing to modify the order.
