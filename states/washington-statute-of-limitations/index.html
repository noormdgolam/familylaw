Understanding Washington's Family Law Statute of Limitations: A Comprehensive Guide

When dealing with family law issues in Washington, it's essential to understand the statute of limitations (SOL) that applies to various legal actions. In this guide, we will delve into the specifics of Washington's SOL for personal injury, property damage, and breach of contract cases, as well as provide insights into the state's family law statute of limitations.

Washington's Statute of Limitations

Washington's SOL is governed by the state's Revised Code (RCW). According to the RCW, the SOL for various legal actions is as follows:

  • Personal Injury: 1 year (RCW 4.16.080)
  • Property Damage: 6 years (RCW 4.16.130)
  • Breach of Contract: 10 years (RCW 4.16.080)

Family Law Statute of Limitations in Washington

While Washington's SOL for personal injury, property damage, and breach of contract cases is well-defined, the state's family law statute of limitations is more complex. The following table provides a comparison of the SOL for various family law actions in Washington:

Family Law Action Statute of Limitations
Divorce No statute of limitations (RCW 26.09.010)
Child Custody No statute of limitations (RCW 26.09.260)
Child Support No statute of limitations (RCW 26.19.030)
Domestic Violence Protection Orders 1 year (RCW 26.50.030)
Parenting Plans No statute of limitations (RCW 26.09.260)

Important Considerations for Family Law Cases

While there is no SOL for divorce, child custody, child support, and parenting plans in Washington, it's essential to note that these cases can still be affected by the state's SOL for other legal actions. For example:

  • Personal Injury: If a family member is injured in an accident, the 1-year SOL for personal injury cases may apply.
  • Property Damage: If a family member's property is damaged, the 6-year SOL for property damage cases may apply.
  • Breach of Contract: If a family member is involved in a contract dispute, the 10-year SOL for breach of contract cases may apply.
