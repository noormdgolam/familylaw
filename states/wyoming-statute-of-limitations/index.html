Wyoming Statute of Limitations for Family Law Cases

The statute of limitations in Wyoming is a critical concept that sets a time limit for filing a lawsuit. The SOL varies depending on the type of case and the nature of the claim. In this section, we will explore the SOL for personal injury, property damage, and breach of contract cases in Wyoming.

Personal Injury Cases in Wyoming

When it comes to personal injury cases in Wyoming, the SOL is typically 1 year. This means that if you have been injured due to someone else's negligence or wrongdoing, you have one year from the date of the incident to file a lawsuit. It is essential to note that this timeframe may be extended in certain circumstances, such as when the injury was not immediately apparent or when the plaintiff was a minor at the time of the incident.

Property Damage Cases in Wyoming

Property damage cases in Wyoming have a longer SOL of 6 years. This means that if your property has been damaged due to someone else's negligence or wrongdoing, you have six years from the date of the incident to file a lawsuit. It is essential to note that this timeframe may be extended in certain circumstances, such as when the damage was not immediately apparent or when the plaintiff was a minor at the time of the incident.

Breach of Contract Cases in Wyoming

Breach of contract cases in Wyoming have a SOL of 5 years. This means that if someone has breached a contract with you, you have five years from the date of the breach to file a lawsuit. It is essential to note that this timeframe may be extended in certain circumstances, such as when the breach was not immediately apparent or when the plaintiff was a minor at the time of the incident.
