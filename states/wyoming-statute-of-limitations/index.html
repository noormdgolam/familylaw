Wyoming Statute of Limitations

The Wyoming statute of limitations governs the time within which a person can bring a lawsuit or file a legal action. In the context of family law, statute of limitations plays a critical role in determining the timeframes for filing divorce, child custody, and child support cases.

Wyoming's statute of limitations varies depending on the specific type of case and the grounds for filing. Here are some key statutes and timeframes to be aware of:

Divorce Statute of Limitations in Wyoming

Wyoming Statute 20-2-103 provides that a divorce can be filed based on "irreconcilable differences" or "no-fault" grounds, but the statute does not specify a time limit for filing. However, if the divorce is filed based on fault grounds, such as adultery, abandonment, or cruel and inhuman treatment, the statute of limitations is two years from the date of the last act of cruel and inhuman treatment.

Wyoming Statute 20-2-104 provides that if a divorce is filed based on irreconcilable differences, the court may grant a divorce if the parties have been living separate and apart for a period of at least six months.

Timeframe for Filing Divorce in Wyoming:

  • No-Fault Divorce: No specific time limit for filing.
  • Fault-Based Divorce: Two years from the date of the last act of cruel and inhuman treatment.

Child Custody Statute of Limitations in Wyoming

Wyoming Statute 20-2-202 provides that a petition for custody of a minor child must be filed within two years from the date of the child's birth or from the date of the child's abandonment.

Timeframe for Filing Child Custody in Wyoming:

  • Initial Custody Petition: Within two years from the date of the child's birth or from the date of the child's abandonment.

Child Support Statute of Limitations in Wyoming

Wyoming Statute 20-6-202 provides that a petition for child support must be filed within three years from the date of the child's majority (age 18) or from the date of the child's emancipation.

Timeframe for Filing Child Support in Wyoming:

  • Initial Child Support Petition: Within three years from the date of the child's majority (age 18) or from the date of the child's emancipation.
