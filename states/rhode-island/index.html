Rhode Island Family Law: A Comprehensive Guide to Divorce and Child Custody

 

As a highly experienced Family Law attorney and legal consultant, I am committed to providing the most up-to-date and authoritative information on Rhode Island Family Law, with a focus on divorce and child custody proceedings.

 

Introduction

Rhode Island's Family Law statutes and procedures are governed by Title 15 of the Rhode Island General Laws (15-1-1 to 15-41-1) and the Rhode Island Family Court Rules. These statutes and rules provide the framework for divorce, child custody, child support, and other family-related matters.

 

Divorce in Rhode Island

Divorce laws in Rhode Island are governed by Chapter 15-5 of the Rhode Island General Laws. To file for divorce in Rhode Island, one or both spouses must have been residents of the state for at least one year prior to the filing of the divorce complaint.

 

Grounds for Divorce

Rhode Island is an "irreconcilable differences" state, which means that a divorce can be granted based on the irreconcilable differences between the spouses, without requiring proof of fault. However, fault-based grounds for divorce are still recognized, including:

 

Grounds for Divorce Description
15-5-1 Dessertion (abandonment)
15-5-2 Crimes against the person (felony)
15-5-3 Adultery
15-5-4 Excessive drunkenness or intoxication, or the habitual use of intoxicating liquors or other drugs to the extent that the person's life has become impaired to such an extent that he or she is unable to support himself or herself or is unable to perform his or her duties as a husband or wife
15-5-5 Imprisonment in a penitentiary or state prison for a term of three (3) or more years

 

Divorce Proceedings

The divorce process in Rhode Island typically begins with the filing of a divorce complaint, which is served on the respondent spouse. The respondent spouse then has 20 days to respond to the complaint. If the respondent spouse does not respond, the court may grant a default judgment in favor of the petitioner spouse.

 

Divorce Forms and Documents

The following forms and documents are typically required in a Rhode Island divorce proceeding:

 

  • Divorce Complaint Form
  • Divorce Answer Form
  • Financial Disclosure Statement (Form #2)
  • Property Settlement Agreement (Form #3)
  • Custody and Visitation Agreement (Form #4)

 

Child Custody in Rhode Island

Child custody laws in Rhode Island are governed by Chapter 15-5.1 of the Rhode Island General Laws. The primary consideration in determining child custody is the best interest of the child.

 

Types of Custody

Rhode Island recognizes the following types of custody:

 

  • Joint Custody: Both parents share legal and physical custody of the child.
  • Sole Custody: One parent has sole legal and physical custody of the child.
  • Shared Custody: Both parents share legal custody, but one parent has primary physical custody and the other parent has visitation rights.
    • li>Split Custody: One parent has custody of one or more children, and the other parent has custody of one or more other children.

 

Child Custody Proceedings

The child custody process in Rhode Island typically begins with the filing of a petition for custody, which is served on the other parent. The court may then schedule a hearing to determine the best interest of the child.

 

Child Custody Evaluation

A child custody evaluation is a comprehensive assessment of the child's needs and the ability of each parent to meet those needs. The evaluation is typically conducted by a neutral third-party expert, such as a psychologist or social worker.

 

Child Support in Rhode Island

Child support laws in Rhode Island are governed by Chapter 15-17 of the Rhode Island General Laws. The primary consideration in determining child support is the needs of the child.

 

Child Support Proceedings

The child support process in Rhode Island typically begins with the filing of a petition for child support, which is served on the other parent. The court may then schedule a hearing to determine the amount of child support.

 

Child Support Forms and Documents

The following forms and documents are typically required in a Rhode Island child support proceeding:

 

  • Child Support Petition
  • Child Support Answer
  • Financial Disclosure Statement

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about Rhode Island Family Law:

 

  • Q: What is the difference between a divorce and a dissolution of marriage? A: In Rhode Island, a divorce is a court-ordered ending of a marriage, while a dissolution of marriage is a mutual agreement by the parties to end the marriage.
  • Q: Can I file for divorce without an attorney? A: Yes, you can file for divorce without an attorney, but it is highly recommended that you seek the advice of an attorney to ensure that your rights are protected.
  • Q: How long does a divorce take? A: The length of time it takes to complete a divorce can vary widely, depending on the complexity of the case and the cooperation of the parties.
  • Q: Can I get full custody of my child? A: In Rhode Island, the court's primary consideration is the best interest of the child. The court may award full custody to one parent, but it is more likely that the court will award joint custody or shared custody.
