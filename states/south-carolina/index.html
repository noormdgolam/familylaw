Family Law, Divorce, and Child Custody in South Carolina: A Comprehensive Guide

 

Introduction

 

Family law, divorce, and child custody are complex and often emotionally charged issues that require careful guidance and expertise. As a highly experienced Family Law attorney and legal consultant, I am committed to providing you with a comprehensive understanding of the laws and procedures governing these matters in South Carolina.

 

State-Specific Statutes

 

The laws governing family law, divorce, and child custody in South Carolina are outlined in the South Carolina Code of Laws. The following statutes are some of the most relevant to these matters:

 

  1. Section 20-3-130: Grounds for Divorce

    2.  

    Ground Description
    1. Adultery The husband or wife has committed adultery.
    2. Desertion The husband or wife has deserted the other for a period of one year or more.
    3. Physical cruelty The husband or wife has been guilty of physical cruelty towards the other spouse.
    4. Mental cruelty The husband or wife has been guilty of mental cruelty towards the other spouse.
    5. Separation The husband and wife have lived separate and apart for a period of one year or more.

     

  2. Section 20-3-150: Custody of Minor Children

    3.  

    Custody Type Description
    Joint Custody The parents share joint custody of the minor children.
    Sole Custody One parent has sole custody of the minor children.
    Split Custody The parents have joint custody of multiple children, but each parent has sole custody of at least one child.

     

  3. Section 20-7-420: Child Support

    4.  

    Child Support Type Description
    Basic Child Support The amount of child support owed by the non-custodial parent, calculated based on the non-custodial parent's income and the number of children.
    Additional Child Support The amount of child support owed by the non-custodial parent for additional expenses, such as medical and education expenses.

 

Common Legal Proceedings

 

The following are some of the most common legal proceedings in a family law, divorce, and child custody case in South Carolina:

 

  • Initiation of Divorce Proceedings

    •  

    The divorce proceedings are initiated by filing a complaint for divorce with the court. The complaint must include the grounds for divorce, the names and ages of any minor children, and the request for custody and child support.

     

  • Service of Process

    •  

    The complaint is served on the respondent by a process server or by certified mail. The respondent has 30 days to respond to the complaint.

     

  • Discovery

    •  

    Both parties must provide discovery, which includes exchanging financial information, witness lists, and other relevant documents.

     

  • Mediation and Arbitration

    •  

    Mediation and arbitration are alternative dispute resolution methods that can be used to resolve disputes outside of court.

     

  • Trial

    •  

    Both parties present their case to the judge, who will make a decision on the issues of custody, child support, and property division.
