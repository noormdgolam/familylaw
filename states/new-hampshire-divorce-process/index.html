Divorce Filing Process and Requirements in New Hampshire: A Comprehensive Guide

In New Hampshire, the divorce process is relatively straightforward, with no residency requirement, waiting period, or fault grounds allowed. This guide will walk you through the necessary steps to file for divorce in the Granite State, highlighting key requirements and legal facts.

Understanding New Hampshire Divorce Laws

New Hampshire is a no-fault divorce state, meaning that the court will grant a divorce based on the grounds of "irreconcilable differences." This allows couples to file for divorce without having to prove fault or wrongdoing by one party.

No Residency Requirement

Unlike many other states, New Hampshire does not require couples to be residents of the state in order to file for divorce. This means that couples who have been living in the state for only a short period of time can still file for divorce.

No Waiting Period

New Hampshire also does not have a waiting period for divorce, which means that couples can file for divorce as soon as they meet the necessary requirements.

The Divorce Filing Process

Step 1: Prepare the Divorce Petition

The first step in the divorce process is to prepare the divorce petition, also known as the complaint. This document outlines the grounds for the divorce, including the reason for the divorce and any relevant details about the marriage.

Step 2: File the Divorce Petition

Once the divorce petition is prepared, it must be filed with the court. In New Hampshire, couples must file the petition with the Circuit Court in the county where either spouse resides.

Step 3: Serve the Other Spouse

After filing the divorce petition, one spouse must serve the other spouse with a copy of the petition. This can be done by a process server, certified mail, or personal service.

Step 4: Respond to the Divorce Petition

The spouse who is served with the divorce petition must respond to the petition within a certain timeframe. In New Hampshire, the respondent has 30 days to file an answer to the petition.

Step 5: Negotiate a Divorce Settlement

Once the respondent has filed an answer to the petition, the parties can begin negotiating a divorce settlement. This may involve mediation, arbitration, or other forms of alternative dispute resolution.

Step 6: Finalize the Divorce

After the parties have reached a divorce settlement, the court will review and approve the agreement. Once the agreement is approved, the court will enter a final divorce decree, formally ending the marriage.

FAQs

Q: Do I need to be a resident of New Hampshire to file for divorce?

A: No, New Hampshire does not require couples to be residents of the state in order to file for divorce.

Q: How long does the divorce process take?

A: The length of time it takes to complete the divorce process varies depending on the complexity of the case and the parties' ability to reach a settlement. In general, the process can take several months to a year or more.

Q: Can I file for divorce on my own?

A: Yes, couples can file for divorce on their own, without the assistance of an attorney. However, it is recommended that couples seek the advice of an attorney to ensure that their rights are protected and their interests are represented.

Q: What is the cost of a divorce in New Hampshire?

A: The cost of a divorce in New Hampshire can vary depending on the complexity of the case and the parties' ability to reach a settlement. In general, the cost of a divorce can range from a few hundred dollars to tens of thousands of dollars.

Q: Can I get a divorce without my spouse's consent?

A: Yes, couples can file for divorce without their spouse's consent. In New Hampshire, couples can file for divorce based on the grounds of "irreconcilable differences," which does not require the consent of the other spouse.
