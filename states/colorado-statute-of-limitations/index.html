Understanding Family Law Statute of Limitations in Colorado

Colorado has a unique set of laws and regulations when it comes to family law, and one critical aspect is the statute of limitations (SOL). In this comprehensive guide, we will delve into the world of family law SOL in Colorado, providing you with the knowledge you need to navigate the complexities of this often-overlooked area of law.

What is the Statute of Limitations?

The statute of limitations is a law that sets a time limit for filing a lawsuit or taking legal action. This time limit varies depending on the type of case and the state in which the case is being filed. In Colorado, the SOL for different types of cases is as follows:

Personal Injury Cases in Colorado

Personal injury cases in Colorado have a SOL of 1 year from the date of the injury. This means that if you or a loved one has been injured due to the negligence of another party, you have 1 year from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit. This includes cases such as:

  • Car accidents
  • Slip and falls
  • Medical malpractice
  • Workplace accidents

Important Considerations for Personal Injury Cases in Colorado

When it comes to personal injury cases in Colorado, there are several important considerations to keep in mind:

  • The SOL begins on the date of the injury, not the date of discovery.
  • The SOL can be tolled (delayed) in certain circumstances, such as if the injured party is a minor or is incapacitated.
  • It's essential to consult with an experienced attorney to determine the best course of action and to ensure that the SOL is not missed.

Property Damage Cases in Colorado

Property damage cases in Colorado have a SOL of 6 years from the date of the damage. This means that if you or a loved one has experienced property damage due to the negligence of another party, you have 6 years from the date of the damage to file a lawsuit. This includes cases such as:

  • Hail damage to vehicles
  • Water damage to homes
  • Fire damage to property
  • Defective products

Breach of Contract Cases in Colorado

Breach of contract cases in Colorado have a SOL of 6 years from the date of the breach.   • Commercial disputes
  • Real estate disputes
  • Business disputes

FAQs

Q: What is the statute of limitations in Colorado?

A: The SOL in Colorado varies depending on the type of case. For personal injury cases, the SOL is 1 year. For property damage cases, the SOL is 6 years. For breach of contract cases, the SOL is also 6 years.

Q: When does the statute of limitations begin to run?

A: The SOL begins to run on the date of the injury, damage, or breach, not the date of discovery.

Q: Can the statute of limitations be tolled?

A: Yes, the SOL can be tolled in certain circumstances, such as if the injured party is a minor or is incapacitated, or if the parties to the contract are deadlocked or dispute the terms of the contract.

Q: What happens if I miss the statute of limitations?

A: If you miss the SOL, you may be barred from filing a lawsuit and may be unable to recover damages. It's essential to consult with an experienced attorney to determine the best course of action and to ensure that the SOL is not missed.
