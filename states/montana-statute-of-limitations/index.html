Understanding Family Law Statute of Limitations in Montana: A Comprehensive Guide

In the state of Montana, family law statute of limitations plays a crucial role in determining the timeframe within which a person can file a lawsuit related to family law issues. In this guide, we will delve into the specific statute of limitations for personal injury, property damage, and breach of contract in Montana, and explore how these limitations apply to family law cases.

Statute of Limitations in Montana

The statute of limitations in Montana is governed by the Montana Code Annotated (MCA). According to the MCA, the following statute of limitations apply:

  • Personal Injury: 2 years (MCA 27-2-203)
  • Property Damage: 2 years (MCA 27-2-205)
  • Breach of Contract: 3 years (MCA 27-2-202)

Applying Statute of Limitations to Family Law Cases

While the statute of limitations mentioned above apply to general personal injury, property damage, and breach of contract cases, family law cases often involve complex and unique circumstances. In Montana, family law cases may include issues such as divorce, child custody, child support, and spousal support. When applying the statute of limitations to these cases, it is essential to consider the specific circumstances and relevant laws.

Divorce and Separation

In Montana, a person may file a petition for divorce or dissolution of marriage within 2 years of the date of separation (MCA 40-4-217). However, if the parties have entered into a separation agreement, the statute of limitations may not apply. It is essential to consult with an attorney to determine the specific requirements and deadlines for your case.

Child Custody and Child Support

The statute of limitations for child custody and child support cases in Montana varies depending on the specific circumstances. Generally, a person may file a petition for child custody or support within 3 years of the date of the child's 18th birthday (MCA 40-4-217). However, if the court has previously made a custody or support order, the statute of limitations may not apply.

Spousal Support

In Montana, a person may file a petition for spousal support within 2 years of the date of the divorce or dissolution of marriage (MCA 40-4-217). However, if the parties have entered into a separation agreement, the statute of limitations may not apply.
