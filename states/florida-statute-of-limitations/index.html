Understanding Family Law Statute of Limitations in Florida

In Florida, the Statute of Limitations (SOL) is a crucial aspect of family law that sets deadlines for filing various legal actions. Understanding these timelines is essential for individuals seeking to initiate a lawsuit or legal proceeding in a Florida court. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the Statute of Limitations in Florida, focusing on family law cases.

The aggregated data for Florida provides the following Statute of Limitations:

  • Personal Injury: 4 years
  • Property Damage: 2 years
  • Breach of Contract: 7 years

Statute of Limitations for Family Law Cases in Florida

Marital Dissolution and Child Custody Cases

In Florida, the Statute of Limitations for marital dissolution (divorce) and child custody cases is not specifically addressed by the aggregated data. However, these cases are typically governed by the Florida Rules of Civil Procedure and the Florida Family Law Rules of Procedure. Generally, these cases can be filed at any time after the marriage or birth of the child, but there may be specific requirements and timelines for filing.

Child Support Cases

According to the Florida Statutes (Section 95.11), the Statute of Limitations for child support cases is 20 years from the date of the last payment. However, this may not apply if the child support obligation was established under a court order or agreement. In such cases, the child support obligation may be enforceable indefinitely.

Domestic Violence Cases

The Statute of Limitations for domestic violence cases in Florida is not specifically addressed by the aggregated data. However, these cases are governed by the Florida Statutes (Chapter 741). Generally, victims of domestic violence can file a petition for a restraining order or injunction at any time after the incident.

Paternity Cases

The Statute of Limitations for paternity cases in Florida is 4 years from the date of birth of the child. This is based on the Florida Statutes (Section 95.11). However, this time limit may be tolled if the putative father is unaware of the child's existence or if the child is not able to be identified.

A: No, the Statute of Limitations for paternity cases in Florida is 4 years from the date of birth of the child. If the child is 18 years old or older, the Statute of Limitations has likely expired.
