Indiana Family Law Statutes

The Indiana Family Law Statutes (Indiana Code Title 31) provide a comprehensive framework for family law in the state. The statutes cover a range of topics, including marriage, divorce, child custody, child support, and adoption. Some of the key statutes include:

  • Indiana Code 31-15-7-4: Residency requirements for divorce - This statute requires that one or both parties must have been a resident of Indiana for at least six months before filing for divorce.
  • Indiana Code 31-15-7-5: Grounds for divorce - This statute lists the grounds for divorce in Indiana, including irreconcilable differences, adultery, and abandonment.
  • Indiana Code 31-17-2-1: Child custody - This statute provides that custody of a child shall be awarded to the parent who has been appointed as the child's custodian by a court order.
  • Indiana Code 31-17-5-3: Child support - This statute provides that a child support obligor shall pay child support to the child's custodian until the child reaches the age of 21, or until the child is emancipated.

Common Family Law Proceedings in Indiana

Family law proceedings in Indiana typically involve one or more of the following:

  • Divorce - A divorce is a legal termination of a marriage. Indiana law provides for both fault-based and no-fault divorces.
  • Child Custody - Child custody refers to the rights and responsibilities of parents to make decisions for their children and to provide a stable home environment.
  • Child Support - Child support is a payment made by one parent to the other parent to support their child's basic needs.
  • Adoption - Adoption is the process of creating a legally recognized parent-child relationship between an adult and a child.
  • Parenting Time - Parenting time refers to the time a child spends with each parent. Indiana law provides a framework for determining parenting time arrangements.

Step-by-Step Guidance on Navigating Family Law in Indiana

Navigating family law in Indiana can be complex and intimidating. Here is a step-by-step guide to help you navigate the system:

  1. Seek Legal Advice - Consult with a family law attorney to discuss your situation and determine the best course of action.
  2. File a Petition - If you decide to pursue a divorce or other family law issue, your attorney will file a petition with the court.
  3. Attend a Hearing - You and your attorney will attend a hearing with the court to discuss your case and determine the outcome.
  4. Finalize the Judgment - If the court grants your petition, you will receive a final judgment outlining the terms of your divorce or other family law issue.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Here are some frequently asked questions about family law in Indiana:

  • Q: What is the residency requirement for divorce in Indiana? A: One or both parties must have been a resident of Indiana for at least six months before filing for divorce.
  • Q: What is the ground for divorce in Indiana? A: The grounds for divorce in Indiana include irreconcilable differences, adultery, and abandonment.
  • Q: How is child custody determined in Indiana? A: Child custody is determined by the court based on the best interests of the child.
  • Q: What is the child support payment schedule in Indiana? A: Child support payments are made on a regular schedule, typically every two weeks.
