Family Law Statute of Limitations in Maryland: A Comprehensive Guide

In Maryland, the statute of limitations (SOL) governs the timeframe within which an individual or entity can file a lawsuit. Understanding these timeframes is crucial for individuals navigating family law disputes, as the failure to file a lawsuit within the specified period can result in the loss of their right to pursue a claim. This guide provides an overview of the statute of limitations in Maryland, focusing on family law-related actions.

Understanding Statute of Limitations in Maryland

General Principles

The statute of limitations is a law that sets a time limit for bringing a lawsuit. It serves to prevent stale claims from being brought to court, which can cause undue delays and prejudice to the defendant. In Maryland, the SOL varies depending on the type of action or claim being pursued.

Statute of Limitations for Family Law-Related Actions

Personal Injury and Property Damage Claims

For personal injury and property damage claims, the SOL in Maryland is 3 years. This means that if an individual is injured or suffers property damage due to the actions of another person or entity, they have 3 years from the date of the incident to file a lawsuit. It's essential to note that this time period may be tolled (or paused) in certain circumstances, such as when the individual is a minor or mentally incapacitated.

Breach of Contract Claims

Breach of contract claims in Maryland have a SOL of 3 years. This means that if a contract is breached, the injured party has 3 years from the date of the breach to file a lawsuit. However, if the breach is discovered later, the SOL may be tolled from the date of discovery.

Special Considerations in Family Law

Divorce and Custody Proceedings

Divorce and custody proceedings in Maryland are not subject to a statute of limitations. However, the court may consider the timeliness of the proceedings in determining the best interests of the child or parties involved.

Child Support and Spousal Support Claims

Child support and spousal support claims in Maryland are not subject to a statute of limitations. However, the court may consider the timeliness of the claim in determining the amount of support owed.
