Family Law Statute of Limitations in Hawaii: A Comprehensive Guide

Understanding the statute of limitations is crucial in family law cases in Hawaii. This guide provides an in-depth look at the relevant laws and regulations, helping you navigate the complexities of family law in the Aloha State.

Statute of Limitations Overview

The statute of limitations is a legal deadline for filing a lawsuit. In Hawaii, the statute of limitations varies depending on the type of case:

  • Personal Injury: 3 years
  • Property Damage: 5 years
  • Breach of Contract: 9 years

Family Law Cases in Hawaii

Family law cases in Hawaii can include divorce, child custody, child support, and property division. The statute of limitations for these cases is generally tied to the type of relief being sought:

  • Divorce: No statute of limitations, but parties must file a complaint within 10 days of serving the summons and petition.
  • Child Custody: No statute of limitations, but parties must file a complaint within a reasonable time after the cause of action arises.
  • Child Support: No statute of limitations, but parties must file a complaint within a reasonable time after the cause of action arises.
  • Property Division: Generally, the statute of limitations for property division is tied to the type of property being divided, but in Hawaii, this is often not an issue as property division is typically addressed in the divorce action.
