Wyoming Family Law Overview

Wyoming is a no-fault divorce state, which means that a divorce can be granted without the need to prove fault or wrongdoing by one spouse. The state's family law statutes are outlined in the Wyoming Statutes, specifically Title 20, which deals with domestic relations.

Wyoming Child Custody Laws

In Wyoming, child custody decisions are based on the "best interests of the child" standard. The court considers several factors when determining custody, including:

  • The wishes of the parents
  • The child's wishes, if the child is old enough to express a preference
  • The child's relationship with each parent
  • The child's adjustment to home, school, and community
  • The mental and physical health of each parent
  • The ability of each parent to provide a stable and loving home environment
  • The history of domestic violence or abuse by either parent
  • The ability of each parent to cooperate and communicate effectively
  • The geographical location of each parent's home

Wyoming Child Support Laws

In Wyoming, child support is based on the guidelines outlined in the Wyoming Child Support Guidelines. The court considers the following factors when determining child support:

  • The income of both parents
  • The number of children involved
  • The ages of the children
  • The needs of the children
  • The ability of each parent to support the children
  • The amount of time each parent spends with the children

Wyoming Divorce Process

The divorce process in Wyoming typically involves the following steps:

  1. Filing a petition for divorce
  2. Serving the other spouse with a summons and a copy of the petition
  3. Waiting for the other spouse to respond to the petition
  4. Engaging in discovery and negotiation
  5. Attending a settlement conference or mediation
  6. Going to trial, if necessary
  7. Finalizing the divorce through a court order

Wyoming Child Custody Process

The child custody process in Wyoming typically involves the following steps:

  1. Both parents filing a petition for custody
  2. The court conducting an investigation and gathering information
  3. The court holding a hearing to determine custody
  4. The court making a decision based on the "best interests of the child" standard
  5. Establishing a custody arrangement and visitation schedule

Wyoming Attorney Costs

The cost of hiring an attorney in Wyoming can vary depending on several factors, including the complexity of the case, the attorney's experience and expertise, and the location of the attorney's office. On average, attorneys in Wyoming charge between $200 and $500 per hour. The total cost of hiring an attorney can range from $1,000 to $10,000 or more, depending on the specifics of the case.

Step-by-Step Guidance for Wyoming Residents

Here are some step-by-step guidelines to help you navigate the family law process in Wyoming:

  1. Contact an attorney: Reach out to a qualified family law attorney in Wyoming to discuss your case and determine the best course of action.
  2. File a petition: If you decide to pursue a divorce or custody case, your attorney will help you file a petition with the court.
  3. Wait for the other spouse to respond: If the other spouse is served with the petition, they will have a certain amount of time to respond.
  4. Engage in discovery and negotiation: Your attorney will help you gather information and negotiate with the other spouse or their attorney.
  5. Attend a settlement conference or mediation: If the parties are unable to reach a settlement, a settlement conference or mediation may be held.
  6. Go to trial, if necessary: If the case cannot be settled, it will go to trial, and a judge will make a decision.
  7. Finalize the divorce or custody order: Once the case is resolved, the court will issue a final order, which will be binding on both parties.
