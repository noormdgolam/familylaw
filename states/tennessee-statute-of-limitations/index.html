Understanding Family Law Statute of Limitations in Tennessee

Tennessee is a state with unique laws governing family matters, and understanding the statute of limitations (SOL) is crucial for individuals navigating the complexities of family law. In this comprehensive guide, we'll delve into the world of family law SOL in Tennessee, exploring the critical deadlines and limitations that apply to various family law actions.

Statute of Limitations for Family Law Actions in Tennessee

The SOL in Tennessee is governed by the Tennessee Code Annotated (TCA). While the TCA provides a general framework for SOL, family law has its own set of specific rules and regulations. Here's a brief overview of the SOL for various family law actions in Tennessee:

Personal Injury Actions

The SOL for personal injury actions in Tennessee is one (1) year from the date of the injury. This means that if you've been injured due to someone else's negligence or wrongdoing, you have one year to file a lawsuit. If you fail to file within this timeframe, you may be barred from seeking compensation.

Property Damage Actions

The SOL for property damage actions in Tennessee is two (2) years from the date of the damage. This includes actions for damage to real or personal property, such as a car accident or a slip-and-fall injury.

Breach of Contract Actions

The SOL for breach of contract actions in Tennessee is seven (7) years from the date of the breach. This means that if someone has breached a contract with you, you have seven years to file a lawsuit to recover damages.

  • Divorce**: The SOL for divorce is not explicitly stated in Tennessee law. However, the court may consider the length of time since the separation and other factors when determining the grounds for divorce.
