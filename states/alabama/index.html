Alabama Family Law Statutes

The Alabama Family Law Code (Title 30, Ala. Code 1975) outlines the rights and responsibilities of family members in various family law matters, including divorce, child custody, child support, and property division. Some key statutes include:

  • Divorce: Ala. Code § 30-2-1 - Alabama allows for both fault-based and no-fault divorces.
  • Child Custody: Ala. Code § 30-3-152 - The Alabama Best Interest of the Child Act (2014) prioritizes the child's best interests in determining custody.
  • Child Support: Ala. Code § 30-3-22 - Alabama follows the Income Shares model to calculate child support
  • Property Division: Ala. Code § 30-2-51 - Alabama is an equitable distribution state, where the court divides marital property fairly but not necessarily equally.

Common Family Law Proceedings in Alabama

Family law cases in Alabama typically follow these common proceedings:

  • Filing a Divorce Petition: Alabama Divorce Petition Form - One spouse files a petition for divorce, citing grounds for the divorce, such as adultery, abandonment, or irreconcilable differences.
  • Temporary Orders: Alabama Temporary Order Form - The court may grant temporary orders for child custody, child support, alimony, and temporary property division.
  • Custody Hearing: Alabama Custody Hearing Form - Both parents present evidence and testimony to determine the child's best interests and the most suitable custody arrangement.
  • Final Decree: Alabama Final Decree Form - The court enters a final decree, which outlines the terms of the divorce, including property division, child custody, and child support.

FAQs on Family Law in Alabama

Here are some frequently asked questions about family law in Alabama:

  • What is the minimum residency requirement for divorce in Alabama? Ala. Code § 30-2-1 - One spouse must have resided in Alabama for at least six months before filing for divorce.
  • How is child support calculated in Alabama? Ala. Code § 30-3-22 - Alabama follows the Income Shares model to calculate child support.
  • Can I change my child's custody arrangement without going to court? - No, any changes to the child's custody arrangement must be approved by the court.
  • Can I file for divorce in Alabama if I'm a non-resident? - Yes, but you must meet the minimum residency requirement (six months) and file the petition in the county where your spouse resides.
  • How long does a divorce take in Alabama? - The length of a divorce in Alabama depends on the complexity of the case and the court's schedule, but it can take several months or even years.
