Common Legal Proceedings in Minnesota Family Law

  • Divorce Proceedings
    • Uncontested divorce: A no-fault divorce where both parties agree on the terms of the divorce
    • Contested divorce: A divorce where the parties disagree on one or more issues, such as custody, property division, or support
  • Child Custody Proceedings
    • Initial custody determination: A court decision on custody and parenting time at the time of the divorce
    • Custody modification: A court order changing the initial custody determination due to changed circumstances
  • Child Support Proceedings
    • Initial child support determination: A court order setting the amount of child support at the time of the divorce
    • Child support modification: A court order changing the initial child support determination due to changed circumstances

Step-by-Step Guidance for Minnesota Family Law Proceedings

  1. Initial Consultation
    1. Contact an experienced family law attorney
    2. Discuss the facts of your case and potential outcomes
    3. Receive guidance on the next steps to take
  2. Uncontested Divorce
    1. Prepare and file the necessary divorce paperwork
    2. Exchange financial information with your spouse
    3. Finalize the divorce decree
  3. Contested Divorce
    1. File the necessary divorce paperwork
    2. Exchange financial information with your spouse
    3. Engage in discovery and negotiation to reach a settlement
    4. Pursue a trial if a settlement is not reached
  4. Custody and Parenting Time Proceedings
    1. File a petition for custody and parenting time
    2. Exchange information and evidence with your spouse
    3. Attend a custody evaluation and/or trial
    4. Receive a court order on custody and parenting time
  5. Child Support Proceedings
    1. File a petition for child support
    2. Exchange information and evidence with your spouse
    3. Attend a hearing and/or trial
    4. Receive a court order on child support

FAQs on Minnesota Family Law

  • Q: How long does a divorce take in Minnesota?
    • A: The length of a divorce in Minnesota can vary depending on the complexity of the case and the level of agreement between the parties.
  • Q: What is the difference between joint and sole custody?
    • A: Joint custody means both parents share decision-making authority and parenting time, while sole custody means one parent has sole decision-making authority and parenting time.
  • Q: How is child support calculated in Minnesota?
    • A: Child support is calculated based on the income of the parents, the number of children, and other factors, using a formula set by the Minnesota Child Support Guidelines.
  • Q: Can I modify a custody or parenting time order in Minnesota?
    • A: Yes, a custody or parenting time order can be modified if there has been a significant change in circumstances that affects the best interests of the child.
