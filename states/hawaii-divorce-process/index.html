State-Specific Statutes for Hawaii Divorce Process

In Hawaii, the divorce process is governed by the Hawaii Revised Statutes (HRS) Title 580, which outlines the grounds for divorce, the procedures for filing for divorce, and the rights and responsibilities of spouses and children.

The HRS Title 580 states that a divorce may be granted on the following grounds:

  • No-fault divorce: The marriage is irretrievably broken, and there is no reasonable likelihood of reconciliation (HRS § 580-10(a))
  • Irreconcilable differences (HRS § 580-10(b))
  • Separation: The spouses have lived separate and apart from each other for at least two years (HRS § 580-10(c))
  • Adultery (HRS § 580-10(d))
  • Physical or mental abuse (HRS § 580-10(e))
  • Other grounds (HRS § 580-10(f))

The HRS Title 580 also outlines the procedures for filing for divorce, which include:

  • Filing a petition for divorce with the court (HRS § 580-12)
  • Serving the petition on the other spouse (HRS § 580-13)
  • Responding to the petition (HRS § 580-14)
  • Mediation (HRS § 580-15)
  • Trials (HRS § 580-16)

Common Legal Proceedings in Hawaii Divorce Process

During the Hawaii divorce process, several common legal proceedings occur, including:

  • Separation Agreement: A written agreement between spouses outlining the terms of their separation, including property division, spousal support, and child custody (HRS § 580-15)
  • Discovery: The exchange of financial information and other relevant documents between spouses, including tax returns, bank statements, and employment records (HRS § 580-16)
  • Motion for Temporary Relief: A request for temporary orders, such as temporary child support, spousal support, or exclusive use of the marital residence (HRS § 580-17)
  • Mediation: A process where a neutral third-party mediator helps spouses reach an agreement on disputed issues (HRS § 580-15)
  • Trials: A court hearing where a judge makes a final decision on disputed issues (HRS § 580-16)

Step-by-Step Guidance for Hawaii Divorce Process

The Hawaii divorce process typically involves the following steps:

  1. Filing a Petition for Divorce: One spouse files a petition for divorce with the court, stating the grounds for divorce and requesting relief (HRS § 580-12)
  2. Serving the Petition: The other spouse is served with the petition, either by personal service or by publication (HRS § 580-13)
  3. Responding to the Petition: The other spouse responds to the petition, either by admitting or denying the allegations and requesting relief (HRS § 580-14)
  4. Mediation: The spouses attend a mediation session, where a neutral third-party mediator helps them reach an agreement on disputed issues (HRS § 580-15)
  5. Discovery: The spouses exchange financial information and other relevant documents, including tax returns, bank statements, and employment records (HRS § 580-16)
  6. Motion for Temporary Relief: The spouses file a motion for temporary orders, such as temporary child support, spousal support, or exclusive use of the marital residence (HRS § 580-17)
  7. Trial: The court holds a trial, where a judge makes a final decision on disputed issues (HRS § 580-16)
  8. Final Judgment: The court enters a final judgment, dissolving the marriage and outlining the terms of the divorce (HRS § 580-20)
