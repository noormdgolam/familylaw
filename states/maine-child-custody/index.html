Understanding Child Custody Laws and Guidelines in Maine

When it comes to child custody, every state has its own set of laws and guidelines. In Maine, the primary factor that determines child custody is the best interests of the child. This guide will provide an in-depth look at the child custody laws and guidelines in Maine, helping you navigate the complex process.

The Best Interests of the Child

The best interests of the child is the primary factor that determines child custody in Maine. This means that the court will make decisions based on what is in the best interest of the child, rather than what is in the best interest of one or both parents.

Factors Considered in Determining the Best Interests of the Child

The court in Maine considers several factors when determining the best interests of the child, including:

  • the wishes of the child
  • the relationship between the child and each parent
  • the ability and willingness of each parent to provide a stable and loving home
  • the child's physical, emotional, and educational needs
  • the availability of resources to meet the child's needs
  • any history of domestic violence or abuse
  • any other relevant factors

Joint Custody in Maine

In Maine, there is a joint custody presumption, which means that the court will start with a presumption that joint custody is in the best interest of the child. However, this presumption can be rebutted if there is evidence that joint custody would not be in the best interest of the child.

What is Joint Custody?

Joint custody means that both parents share physical and legal custody of the child. This can include joint decision-making, joint physical custody, or a combination of both.

Age of the Child in Custody Decisions

In Maine, the age of the child is a factor in custody decisions. If the child is 14 or older, the court will consider the child's wishes when making decisions about custody.

How Does the Child's Age Affect Custody Decisions?

The court will consider the child's age when making decisions about custody, but the court is not obligated to follow the child's wishes. The court will still consider the child's wishes, but will also consider the best interests of the child.

Custody Actions and Requirements in Maine

Action/Requirement Description
Filing a Petition The first step in seeking custody is to file a petition with the court.

Discovery Both parents will have the opportunity to gather information about the other parent's lifestyle, income, and other relevant factors.
Custody Evaluation The court may order a custody evaluation to help determine the best interests of the child.

Custody Order The court will make a decision about custody based on the evidence presented.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the best way to file for custody in Maine? A: The best way to file for custody in Maine is to consult with an attorney who specializes in family law. They can guide you through the process and ensure that your petition is properly filed.

Q: Do I need a court order to move out of state with my child? A: Yes, you will need a court order to move out of state with your child. The court will consider the best interests of the child and the potential impact on the child's relationship with each parent before making a decision.

Q: Can I modify a custody order in Maine? A: Yes, you can modify a custody order in Maine, but you will need to show a significant change in circumstances that warrant a change in custody.

Q: What is the age of majority in Maine? A: The age of majority in Maine is 18.

Q: Can I have joint custody of my child if we are not married? A: Yes, you can have joint custody of your child if you are not married. In Maine, joint custody is available to both married and unmarried parents.
