Divorce Filing Process and Requirements in Oklahoma: A Comprehensive Guide

In Oklahoma, the divorce process is governed by state laws that dictate the necessary steps and requirements for couples seeking to end their marriage. This guide provides an in-depth look at the divorce filing process and requirements in Oklahoma, helping individuals navigate this complex and often emotional process.

Eligibility and Residency Requirements

Oklahoma does not require a specific residency period for individuals seeking a divorce. This means that either spouse can file for divorce without meeting a minimum residency requirement, provided they meet the other necessary qualifications.

The Divorce Process in Oklahoma

Step 1: Determine Grounds for Divorce

Oklahoma is a no-fault divorce state, meaning that couples do not need to establish fault on the part of the other spouse to obtain a divorce. In Oklahoma, divorce is based solely on irreconcilable differences, which can be a valid reason for divorce without any additional requirements.

Step 2: File a Petition for Divorce

To initiate the divorce process, one spouse must file a Petition for Divorce with the court in the county where they reside. The petition must include basic information about the couple, including their names, addresses, and the grounds for divorce.

Step 3: Serve the Other Spouse

After filing the petition, the spouse who initiated the divorce must serve the other spouse with a copy of the petition and a summons. This can be done through personal service or by certified mail.

Step 4: Waiting Period

Oklahoma requires a 30-day waiting period between the time the petition is filed and the time the divorce is finalized. This waiting period gives both spouses an opportunity to reconcile or to make arrangements for their children and property.

Divorce Forms and Requirements

Required Documents

The following documents are typically required for a divorce in Oklahoma:

  • Petition for Divorce (Form 11-1)
  • Summons (Form 11-2)
  • Verification (Form 11-3)
  • Military Affidavit (if applicable)
  • Property Settlement Agreement (if applicable)
  • Parenting Plan (if applicable)

Divorce Options in Oklahoma

Court-Ordered Divorce

A court-ordered divorce is the most common type of divorce in Oklahoma. This type of divorce involves a court hearing where a judge makes decisions about property division, child custody, and other issues.

Uncontested Divorce

An uncontested divorce occurs when both spouses agree on all issues, including property division and child custody. This type of divorce is often faster and less expensive than a court-ordered divorce.

Mediation

Mediation is a process where a neutral third party helps couples negotiate and reach agreements on issues such as property division and child custody. Mediation can be a cost-effective and efficient way to resolve divorce issues.

Child Custody and Support in Oklahoma

Types of Custody

Oklahoma recognizes two types of custody:

  • Sole custody: One parent has primary physical and legal custody of the child.
  • Joint custody: Both parents share physical and legal custody of the child.

Child Support

Oklahoma uses the income shares method to calculate child support. This method takes into account both parents' incomes and expenses to determine the amount of child support owed.

Property Division in Oklahoma

Types of Property

Oklahoma recognizes two types of property:

  • Separate property: Property that is not jointly owned, such as individual assets and debts.
  • Community property: Property that is jointly owned, such as marital assets and debts.

Property Division

In Oklahoma, property division is typically based on the principle of equitable distribution. This means that property is divided fairly and in a way that is considered just and reasonable.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: Do I need a lawyer to get a divorce in Oklahoma?

A: While it is not required to have a lawyer, having one can help ensure that your rights are protected and that the divorce process is handled correctly.

Q: How long does it take to get a divorce in Oklahoma?

A: The length of time it takes to get a divorce in Oklahoma depends on the complexity of the case and the level of cooperation between the spouses. However, the minimum waiting period is 30 days.

Q: Can I get a divorce if my spouse is deployed or in the military?

A: Yes, it is possible to get a divorce
