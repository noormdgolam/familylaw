Divorce Filing Process and Requirements in Wyoming

Divorce can be a complex and emotionally challenging process, especially when navigating the intricacies of state-specific laws. In Wyoming, the divorce filing process is relatively straightforward, with no residency requirement, waiting period, or complex grounds for fault-based divorces. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the requirements and steps involved in filing for divorce in Wyoming.

Grounds for Divorce in Wyoming

Wyoming is a no-fault divorce state, but grounds for fault-based divorces are also allowed. The grounds for fault-based divorces in Wyoming include:

  • Adultery
  • Abandonment
  • Cruelty
  • Imprisonment
  • Extreme and repeated mental abuse
  • Extreme and repeated physical abuse that results in serious bodily harm
  • Conviction of a felony and subsequent imprisonment for one year or more
  • Willful neglect or failure to provide support

Filing for Divorce in Wyoming

Wyoming does not have a residency requirement for divorce, meaning that either spouse can file for divorce in the state without needing to be a resident. To file for divorce in Wyoming, you will need to gather the necessary documents and follow the steps outlined below:

Step 1: Determine the Type of Divorce

Wyoming allows two types of divorces: contested and uncontested. An uncontested divorce is one in which both spouses agree on all issues, including property division, child custody, and spousal support. A contested divorce, on the other hand, involves disputes over one or more issues.

Step 2: Gather Required Documents

To file for divorce in Wyoming, you will need to gather the following documents:

  • Divorce petition (form C-10)
  • Summons (form C-11)
  • Divorce complaint (form C-12)
  • Financial affidavit (form C-13)
  • Divorce decree (form C-14)

Wyoming Divorce Filing Requirements

The following table outlines the requirements for filing for divorce in Wyoming:

Document Description Required Information
Divorce Petition (Form C-10) A petition filed by one spouse to initiate the divorce proceedings. Name, address, and date of birth of both spouses, grounds for divorce, and requested relief.
Summons (Form C-11) A formal notice issued to the other spouse informing them of the divorce proceedings. Name and address of the spouse being served, date and time of the divorce hearing, and a statement of the grounds for divorce.
Divorce Complaint (Form C-12) A complaint filed by one spouse to initiate the divorce proceedings. Name, address, and date of birth of both spouses, grounds for divorce, and requested relief.
Financial Affidavit (Form C-13) A sworn statement of financial information filed by one or both spouses.
Divorce Decree (Form C-14) A final judgment entered by the court granting the divorce.

Divorce Process in Wyoming

Once you have gathered the necessary documents, you will need to file them with the court and serve the other spouse with the summons and divorce complaint. The divorce process in Wyoming typically proceeds as follows:

Step 1: Filing of Divorce Petition

The petition for divorce is filed with the court, and a summons is issued to the other spouse.

Step 2: Service of Summons and Divorce Complaint

The other spouse is served with the summons and divorce complaint, which informs them of the divorce proceedings and the grounds for divorce.

Step 3: Response to Divorce Petition

The other spouse has the opportunity to respond to the divorce petition and contest the grounds for divorce.

Step 4: Divorce Hearing

A hearing is held before a judge to determine the terms of the divorce, including property division, child custody, and spousal support.

Step 5: Entry of Divorce Decree

The court enters a final judgment granting the divorce, and the divorce decree is issued.

The following table outlines the key laws governing divorce in Wyoming:
