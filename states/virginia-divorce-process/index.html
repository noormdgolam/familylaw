Divorce Filing Process and Requirements in Virginia: A Comprehensive Guide

Are you considering filing for divorce in Virginia? Understanding the process and requirements can help you navigate this challenging time with confidence. In this guide, we will walk you through the divorce filing process and requirements in Virginia, providing you with the information you need to make informed decisions.

The Divorce Filing Process in Virginia

The divorce filing process in Virginia is relatively straightforward, but it requires attention to detail and a clear understanding of the requirements. Here's an overview of the process:

Step 1: Meet the Residency Requirement

In Virginia, there is no residency requirement for filing for divorce. This means that you can file for divorce in any county where you or your spouse resides.

Step 2: Choose the Type of Divorce

Virginia is a no-fault divorce state, which means that you do not need to prove fault or wrongdoing to get a divorce. However, you can choose to file for divorce on grounds of:

  • No-fault divorce (also known as "irreconcilable differences")

Step 3: Prepare and File the Divorce Petition

To file for divorce, you will need to prepare a divorce petition, which is a document that outlines the reasons for your divorce and requests a divorce decree. You will need to file the petition with the circuit court in the county where you or your spouse resides.

Step 4: Serve the Divorce Petition

After filing the divorce petition, you will need to serve it on your spouse, either in person or through a process server. Your spouse will have 30 days to respond to the petition.

Step 5: Wait for the 60-Day Waiting Period

After serving the divorce petition, you will need to wait for 60 days before the court can grant a divorce decree. This waiting period is intended to give both parties time to settle any outstanding issues and come to a mutually agreeable settlement.

Step 6: Attend a Divorce Hearing

After the waiting period has expired, you will need to attend a divorce hearing, where a judge will review your divorce petition and determine whether to grant a divorce decree.

Requirements for Filing for Divorce in Virginia

In addition to the steps outlined above, there are several requirements that you will need to meet when filing for divorce in Virginia:

Grounds for Divorce

As mentioned earlier, Virginia is a no-fault divorce state, which means that you do not need to prove fault or wrongdoing to get a divorce. However, you can choose to file for divorce on grounds of:

  • No-fault divorce (also known as "irreconcilable differences")

Waiting Period

The waiting period for divorce in Virginia is 60 days. This means that you cannot get a divorce decree until at least 60 days have passed since the divorce petition was served on your spouse.

Service of Process

You will need to serve the divorce petition on your spouse, either in person or through a process server. Your spouse will have 30 days to respond to the petition.
