Family Law Attorney Child Custody Guide in Idaho

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Child custody is a critical aspect of family law in Idaho, and as a Family Law attorney, it is essential to understand the state's specific statutes, common legal proceedings, and the expected attorney costs associated with obtaining or fighting for custody. In this comprehensive article, we will guide you through the process, provide step-by-step guidance, and address frequently asked questions (FAQs) about child custody in Idaho.

Idaho Statutes Governing Child Custody

The Idaho Legislature has established specific statutes governing child custody, which can be found in the Idaho Code, Title 32, Chapter 705. Some key provisions include:

Section Provision Explanation
32-705(1) Best interests of the child The court shall determine the custody of a child according to the best interests of the child.
32-705(2) Factors to consider The court shall consider the following factors in determining the best interests of the child:
    (a) Wishes of the parents The court shall consider the wishes of the parents, but the wishes of the parents shall not be given controlling weight.
    (b) Wishes of the child The court shall consider the wishes of the child, taking into account the child's age and maturity.
    (c) Parent-child relationship The court shall consider the relationship between the child and each parent.
    (d) Ability of parents to cooperate The court shall consider the ability of parents to cooperate in the care and upbringing of the child.
    (e) Domestic violence The court shall consider evidence of domestic violence in determining the best interests of the child.
    (f) History of substance abuse The court shall consider evidence of substance abuse by either parent.
    (g) Custody plan The court shall consider the parents' proposed custody plan.

Types of Custody in Idaho

There are two primary types of custody in Idaho: physical custody and legal custody.

Physical Custody: Physical custody refers to the day-to-day care and supervision of the child. The parent with physical custody is responsible for providing daily care, including feeding, clothing, and education.

Legal Custody: Legal custody refers to the right to make decisions about the child's education, healthcare, and welfare. Legal custody can be shared jointly or awarded to one parent.

Common Legal Proceedings in Idaho Child Custody Cases

The following are some common legal proceedings in Idaho child custody cases:

  • Initial Custody Action: The initial custody action is a petition filed by one parent seeking custody of the child. The petition must include a proposed custody plan and a statement of the reasons for the requested custody arrangement.
  • Custody Evaluation: A custody evaluation is a comprehensive assessment of the child's best interests. A court-appointed evaluator will conduct interviews, home visits, and psychological assessments to determine the child's needs and preferences.
  • Custody Hearing: A custody hearing is a trial where the court will hear evidence and testimony from both parents and may appoint a custody evaluator to provide a recommendation.
  • Custody Order: A custody order is a court-issued document outlining the terms of custody, including physical and legal custody arrangements.

Expected Attorney Costs in Idaho Child Custody Cases

The expected attorney costs in Idaho child custody cases can vary widely, depending on the complexity of the case, the number of court appearances, and the level of involvement required. Here are some estimated costs:

  • Initial Consultation: $200-$500
  • Custody Petition Filing: $500-$1,000
  • Custody Evaluation: $2,000-$5,000
  • Custody Hearing: $3,000-$10,000
  • Custody Order Preparation: $1,000-$3,000
  • Total Estimated Cost: $7,700-$23,500

Step-by-Step Guidance for Parents Seeking Custody in Idaho

Here is a step-by-step guide for parents seeking custody in Idaho:

  1. Consult with an attorney: Meet with a qualified Family Law attorney to discuss your case and determine the best course of action.
  2. File a custody petition: Have your attorney file a custody petition with the court, outlining your proposed custody plan and reasons for seeking custody.
  3. Participate in a custody evaluation: Cooperate with the court-appointed evaluator to provide information and answer questions about your parenting style and relationship with your child.
  4. Attend a custody hearing: Appear in court to present your case and argue for the best interests of your child.
  5. Obtain a custody order: Work with your attorney to prepare a custody order outlining the terms of custody, including physical and legal custody arrangements.

FAQs About Child Custody in Idaho

Here are some frequently asked questions about child custody in Idaho:

  • Q: How long does a custody case take in Idaho? A: The length of a custody case in Idaho can vary widely, depending on the complexity of the case and the court's schedule. On average, a custody case can take several months to a year or more to resolve.
  • Q: Can I request joint custody in Idaho? A: Yes, you can request joint custody in Idaho, but the court will consider the best interests of the child and may award joint custody only if it is in the child's best interests.
  • Q: Can I move out of state with my child in Idaho? A: No, you cannot move out of state with your child without the other parent's consent or a court order. Idaho law prohibits parents from relocating with their children without the other parent's consent or a court order.
  • Q: What are the grounds for modifying a custody order in Idaho? A: The grounds for modifying a custody order in Idaho include a change in circumstances, such as a significant change in work schedule, a move to a different state, or a deterioration in the relationship between the child and one parent.
