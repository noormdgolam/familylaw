Family Law Attorney Child Custody in New Hampshire

 

New Hampshire is a state with a unique approach to family law, particularly when it comes to child custody. As a highly experienced family law attorney, it's essential to understand the state-specific statutes, common legal proceedings, and expected attorney costs to navigate the complexities of child custody in New Hampshire.

 

State-Specific Statutes

 

According to RSA 458:1, child custody in New Hampshire is governed by the following principles:

Principle Description
Best Interests of the Child The court's primary consideration in determining child custody is the best interests of the child.
Custody Awarded to Both Parents The court may award joint custody to both parents, with one parent having primary physical custody and the other having visitation rights.
Custody Awarded to One Parent The court may award sole custody to one parent, with the other parent having visitation rights.
Parental Rights The court may restrict or terminate parental rights, including visitation rights, if a parent is deemed unfit or poses a risk to the child's well-being.

 

Common Legal Proceedings

 

Family law attorneys in New Hampshire typically handle the following common legal proceedings related to child custody:

  • Initial Child Custody Hearing: The first court hearing to determine child custody, where both parents present their cases and provide evidence to support their claim.
  • Child Custody Evaluation: A court-appointed professional conducts an evaluation to assess the child's needs and determine the most suitable custody arrangement.
  • Modification of Custody Order: A court hearing to modify an existing custody order, such as a change in primary residence or visitation schedule.
  • Emergency Custody Petition: An urgent court hearing to address a child's immediate safety concerns, such as a parent's substance abuse or neglect.

Step-by-Step Guidance

 

To navigate the child custody process in New Hampshire, follow these steps:

  1. Seek Legal Advice: Consult with a family law attorney to understand your rights and options.
  2. File a Petition: Submit a petition for child custody to the court, including supporting documentation and evidence.
  3. Attend a Hearing: Participate in a court hearing to present your case and provide evidence.
  4. Cooperate with the Court: Follow the court's orders and participate in any required evaluations or assessments.
  5. Review and Modify the Custody Order: If necessary, file a petition to modify the existing custody order.

 

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

 

Here are some frequently asked questions about child custody in New Hampshire:

  • Q: Can I file for child custody without an attorney?
  • A: It is highly recommended to hire an attorney to ensure your rights are protected and your case is presented effectively.
  • Q: How long does a child custody hearing take?
  • A: The length of a child custody hearing can vary depending on the complexity of the case and the court's schedule.
  • Q: Can I change my child custody arrangement?
  • A: Yes, you can file a petition to modify the existing custody order, but you must demonstrate a substantial change in circumstances.
  • Q: How will the court determine the best interests of the child?
  • A: The court will consider various factors, including the child's physical, emotional, and educational needs, as well as the parents' ability to provide a stable and loving environment.
