Introduction to Child Custody in New Hampshire

In New Hampshire, child custody is governed by the state's statutes and court rules. The primary goal of the court is to determine the best interests of the child, which involves considering various factors such as the child's physical, emotional, and psychological well-being. The court's decision is based on the specific circumstances of each case, and the outcome may vary depending on the unique needs and circumstances of the family.

New Hampshire Child Custody Statutes

New Hampshire Revised Statutes Annotated (RSA) Chapter 458 provides the framework for child custody proceedings in the state. The statutes outline the factors that the court must consider when determining child custody, including:

  • The relationship between the child and each parent
  • The ability of each parent to provide a stable and loving environment
  • The child's physical, emotional, and psychological needs
  • The child's preference, if the child is of sufficient age and maturity
  • Any history of domestic violence or abuse
  • The geographic location of each parent's residence and the potential impact on the child's life

Types of Child Custody in New Hampshire

There are several types of child custody arrangements that may be awarded in New Hampshire, including:

  • Sole Custody: One parent has primary physical and legal custody of the child
  • Joint Custody: Both parents share physical and/or legal custody of the child
  • Shared Custody: Both parents have equal or nearly equal physical custody of the child
  • Split Custody: Each parent has sole custody of one or more children

Common Legal Proceedings in New Hampshire Child Custody Cases

In New Hampshire, child custody cases typically involve the following legal proceedings:

  • Filing a Petition: One or both parents file a petition with the court to establish or modify child custody
  • Temporary Orders: The court may issue temporary orders to establish a custody arrangement pending the final determination
  • Discovery: Both parties exchange information and evidence to support their custody claims
  • Mediation: The parties may participate in mediation to attempt to reach a settlement
  • Trial: If a settlement is not reached, the case will proceed to trial, where the court will make a final determination

Step-by-Step Guidance for New Hampshire Child Custody Cases

The following steps provide a general outline of the child custody process in New Hampshire:

  1. Consult with a Family Law attorney to discuss your case and determine the best course of action
  2. Gather evidence and information to support your custody claim, including documents, witnesses, and expert testimony
  3. File a petition with the court to establish or modify child custody
  4. Participate in temporary order hearings, discovery, and mediation as necessary
  5. Prepare for trial by organizing your evidence and testimony
  6. Attend the trial and present your case to the court
  7. Receive the court's final determination and modify the custody arrangement as necessary

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) About New Hampshire Child Custody

The following FAQs provide additional information and guidance on child custody in New Hampshire:

  • Q: What is the best interests of the child standard in New Hampshire?
    A: The best interests of the child standard requires the court to consider the child's physical, emotional, and psychological well-being when determining child custody.
  • Q: Can I modify a child custody order in New Hampshire?
    A: Yes, a child custody order can be modified in New Hampshire if there has been a significant change in circumstances since the original order was issued.
  • Q: Do I need to hire a Family Law attorney to handle my child custody case in New Hampshire?
    A: While it is not required to hire a Family Law attorney, it is highly recommended to ensure that your rights and interests are protected.
  • Q: How long does a child custody case take to resolve in New Hampshire?
    A: The length of time it takes to resolve a child custody case in New Hampshire can vary depending on the complexity of the case and the court's schedule, but it can take several months to several years.
  • Q: Can I appeal a child custody decision in New Hampshire?
    A: Yes, a child custody decision can be appealed in New Hampshire, but the appeal must be filed within a certain time period and must be based on a specific error or injustice.
