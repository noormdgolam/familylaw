Family Law Attorney Child Custody Guide in Maine

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Maine is a state with unique laws and regulations regarding child custody. As a family law attorney, understanding these laws and procedures is crucial for any individual navigating the complex world of child custody in Maine.

Overview of Maine's Child Custody Laws

Maine's child custody laws are governed by Title 19-A, Maine Revised Statutes, Annotated (M.R.S.A.). This chapter outlines the rights and responsibilities of parents and other caregivers in matters related to child custody.

Types of Child Custody Arrangements

Maine recognizes several types of child custody arrangements, including:

  • Physical Custody: Refers to the physical care and supervision of the child.
  • Legal Custody: Refers to the right to make major decisions regarding the child's life, including education, healthcare, and religion.
  • Joint Custody: Both parents share physical and/or legal custody of the child.
  • Sole Custody: One parent has physical and/or legal custody of the child.
  • Custody by Agreement: Parents agree on a custody arrangement, which may be formalized through a court order or a written agreement.

Best Interests of the Child Standard

The "best interests of the child" standard is the primary consideration in determining child custody arrangements in Maine. This standard is outlined in M.R.S.A. § 19-A-121. The court considers factors such as:

  • The love, affection, and other emotional ties between the child and each parent.
  • The child's wishes, taking into account the child's age and maturity.
  • The child's physical, emotional, and educational needs.
  • The parents' ability to provide a stable and loving environment.
  • The child's relationship with siblings and other relatives.
  • The parents' willingness to cooperate and communicate with each other.
  • The parents' ability to provide for the child's physical, emotional, and educational needs.
  • The parents' history of domestic violence, substance abuse, or other behaviors that may impact the child's well-being.

Child Custody Proceedings in Maine

Child custody proceedings in Maine typically involve the following steps:

Step 1: Filing a Petition

The first step in a child custody proceeding is to file a petition with the court. This petition typically includes:

  • The party's name and address.
  • A description of the child's name, age, and address.
  • A request for the type of relief sought, such as custody, visitation, or support.
  • A statement of the facts supporting the request.

Step 2: Service of Process

Once the petition is filed, the court will issue a summons and schedule a hearing date. The serving party must serve the opposing party with the summons and a copy of the petition.

Step 3: Discovery

Discovery is the process of gathering information and evidence to support a party's case. This may include:

  • Interrogatories: Written questions that must be answered in writing.
  • Requests for Production: Requests for documents, records, or other evidence.
  • Depositions: Sworn testimony of a party or witness.
  • Expert Witness Testimony: Testimony from a professional expert, such as a psychologist or social worker.

Step 4: Mediation and Settlement

Many child custody proceedings involve mediation and settlement. Mediation is a process where a neutral third-party helps parties reach a mutually acceptable agreement. Settlement occurs when parties agree to a resolution outside of court.

Step 5: Trial

If mediation and settlement are unsuccessful, the case will proceed to trial. At trial, parties present their evidence and testimony to a judge or jury, who will make a decision regarding child custody.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about child custody in Maine:

Q: What is the best way to resolve a child custody dispute in Maine?

A: The best way to resolve a child custody dispute in Maine is through mediation and settlement. This allows parties to reach a mutually acceptable agreement without the need for a trial.

Q: How long does a child custody proceeding in Maine typically take?

A: The length of a child custody proceeding in Maine can vary depending on the complexity of the case and the parties' willingness to cooperate. On average, a proceeding may take several months to a year or more to resolve.

Q: Can I represent myself in a child custody proceeding in Maine?

A: Yes, you can represent yourself in a child custody proceeding in Maine. However, it is highly recommended that you hire a family law attorney to guide you through the process and ensure that your rights are protected.

Q: What is the role of a guardian ad litem in a child custody proceeding in Maine?

A: A guardian ad litem is a neutral third-party appointed by the court to represent the best interests of the child. They may investigate the parties' circumstances, interview witnesses, and provide recommendations to the court.

Q: Can I modify a child custody order in Maine?

A: Yes, you can modify a child custody order in Maine. To do so, you must file a petition for modification and demonstrate a substantial change in circumstances that affects the child's best interests.
