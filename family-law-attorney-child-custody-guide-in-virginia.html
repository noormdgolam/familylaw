Family Law Attorney Child Custody Guide in Virginia

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Family law attorney handling child custody cases in Virginia requires in-depth understanding of the Commonwealth's unique statutes and case law governing child custody matters, including the provisions of the Virginia Code that outline the factors guiding a court's discretion in awarding custody of a child.

The primary objectives of the Virginia child custody law are to ensure the best interests of the child and provide for a safe and stable environment for the child's life. The statute explicitly provides that courts should make arrangements regarding the custody of a child that best promote the child's interests, safety, physical, mental, and emotional health, and welfare.

The family law attorney representing the custodial parent must be knowledgeable of the Virginia Code, which lists twelve factors that the court shall consider in determining an appropriate custody arrangement. These factors include, but are not limited to, the child's wishes regarding the allocation of parental responsibilities, the child's present and future needs, the parents' past performance and ability to effectively and lovingly care for the minor child, both individually and together, in the past, and the extent to which parents work in unison in matters affecting the child's life.

It is crucial to note that the Virginia Code emphasizes a preference for the maximum involvement and continuing post-divorce relationship between a child and both parents, if possible. Additionally, the court in child custody proceedings may also consider any relevant evidence, not contained in the Virginia Code, which is deemed pertinent to ensuring the child's best interests are met.

Virginia is an equitable distribution state, which means that the assets and property acquired during the marriage will be divided between the spouses in a manner that is reasonable and fair, according to the circumstances of the parties and the best interests of the child.

Furthermore, family law attorneys must also be knowledgeable about post-divorce orders and the procedures for modifying them in Virginia. The Virginia Code outlines the requirements for requesting modifications to an existing custody order. A party seeking to modify a custody arrangement must show a material and substantial change in circumstances that occurred since the last order was made.

Moreover, attorneys representing clients dealing with Virginia child custody matters also need to have knowledge of international child abduction laws, including the implementation of the Hague Convention, particularly in cases involving a child born in or taken out of the United States without the other parent's consent.

In resolving child custody disputes, family law attorneys may employ a variety of tactics, including litigation, mediation, and collaborative law. Effective communication between all parties is essential to finding common ground and creating a resolution that best suits the child's interests.

The role of a skilled family law attorney in handling child custody cases in Virginia is critical to securing the well-being and health of a child. It is crucial that such an attorney can effectively navigate the complexities of child custody law, while providing a voice for their client.

A family law attorney in Virginia must be aware of the many different types of custody arrangements available, including joint physical and joint legal custody, where both parents share parental authority, with joint physical custody meaning the child spends equal time in each parent's home and joint legal custody meaning both parents have the right to make the important decisions regarding education, healthcare, and extracurricular activities.

Ultimately, a family law attorney experienced in Virginia child custody law offers a high level of expertise and dedication, which will serve as the foundation for protecting and promoting the best interests of the child in the case.
