Family Law Attorney Child Custody Guide in Louisiana

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Family law attorneys in Louisiana who specialize in child custody cases understand the emotional and stressful nature of these proceedings. The state's laws and court system aim to prioritize the best interests of the child, but the process can be complex and challenging for parents involved. This article provides an in-depth look at the laws and procedures surrounding child custody in Louisiana, including the role of family law attorneys, common legal proceedings, costs, and frequently asked questions.

Types of Child Custody in Louisiana

In Louisiana, there are two main types of child custody: sole custody and joint custody.

  • Sole Custody: This is when one parent has the sole responsibility for making decisions regarding the child's care and well-being. The non-custodial parent may still have visitation rights, but their involvement in major decisions is limited.
  • Joint Custody: This is when both parents share decision-making authority and responsibilities for the child. Joint custody can be further divided into:
    • Joint Physical Custody: Both parents share physical custody of the child, with the child splitting time between both homes.
    • Joint Legal Custody: Both parents share decision-making authority, but one parent may still have primary physical custody.
    • Joint Physical and Legal Custody: Both parents share both physical and decision-making authority.

Determining the Best Interests of the Child

The courts in Louisiana prioritize the best interests of the child when making custody decisions. This is determined by considering several factors, including:

  • The wishes of the child (if they are old enough to express a preference)
  • The relationship between the child and each parent
  • The child's adjustment to their home, school, and community
  • The mental and physical health of each parent
  • The parents' ability to provide a stable and loving home environment
  • Any history of domestic violence or abuse
  • The child's cultural and ethnic background

Establishing Parental Rights and Responsibilities

To establish parental rights and responsibilities, parties may need to file a petition with the court. This can be done through a:

  • Petition for Joint Custody
  • Petition for Sole Custody
  • Petition for Modification of Custody Order

The petition should include:

  • Information about the child's current living arrangements
  • The parents' current relationship status
  • The child's wishes (if applicable)
  • Any relevant medical or psychological evaluations

Common Legal Proceedings in Child Custody Cases

Child custody cases in Louisiana may involve several legal proceedings, including:

  • Mediation: A neutral third-party facilitates a discussion between the parties to reach a mutually acceptable agreement.
  • Custody Evaluation: A court-appointed expert evaluates the child's best interests and makes recommendations to the court.
  • Hearing: A court hearing where the parties present evidence and arguments to the judge.
  • Trial: A formal court proceeding where the parties present evidence and witnesses to the judge.

Step-by-Step Guidance for Parents Involved in Child Custody Cases

Parents involved in child custody cases in Louisiana can follow these steps to navigate the process:

  1. Seek legal representation from a qualified family law attorney.
  2. Gather relevant documents and information, including:
    • Birth certificates
    • Marriage certificates
    • Divorce decrees (if applicable)
    • Medical and psychological evaluations (if applicable)
  3. File a petition with the court, outlining the desired custody arrangement.
  4. Participate in mediation or other alternative dispute resolution methods.
  5. Prepare for and attend a custody evaluation (if ordered by the court).
  6. Attend court hearings and trials to present evidence and arguments.
  7. Follow the court's orders and decisions regarding custody.

Expected Attorney Costs in Child Custody Cases

The cost of hiring a family law attorney in Louisiana can vary depending on several factors, including:

  • The attorney's experience and reputation
  • The complexity of the case
  • The number of hearings and court appearances

On average, parents can expect to pay between $200-$500 per hour for attorney services. The total cost can range from $2,000-$10,000 or more, depending on the specifics of the case. Some attorneys may also charge a flat fee for certain services, such as mediation or custody evaluations.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Here are some frequently asked questions about child custody in Louisiana:

Q: What is the difference between joint custody and sole custody?

A: Joint custody involves both parents sharing decision-making authority and responsibilities for the child, while sole custody is when one parent has the sole responsibility for making decisions regarding the child's care and well-being.

Q: How do I establish parental rights and responsibilities?

A: To establish parental rights and responsibilities, parties may need to file a petition with the court, which can be done through a petition for joint custody, sole custody, or modification of custody order.

Q: What factors do the courts consider when making custody decisions?

A: The courts in Louisiana prioritize the best interests of the child when making custody decisions. This is determined by considering several factors, including the wishes of the child, the relationship between the child and each parent, the child's adjustment to their home, school, and community, and the mental and physical health of each parent.

Q: How long does a child custody case typically take?

A: The length of a child custody case can vary depending on the complexity of the case and the number of hearings and court appearances. On average, cases can take several months to a year or more to resolve.

Q: Can I represent myself in a child custody case?

A: While it is possible to represent yourself in a child custody case, it is highly recommended that you seek the assistance of a qualified family law attorney. They can help you navigate the process, gather evidence, and present your case in court.

Conclusion

Child custody cases in Louisiana can be complex and emotionally challenging for parents involved. By understanding the laws and procedures surrounding child custody, parents can better navigate the process and make informed decisions about their child's care and well-being. If you are involved in a child custody case, consider seeking the assistance of a qualified family law attorney to help you achieve the best possible outcome for your child.
