Family Law Attorney Child Custody in Wyoming - A Comprehensive Overview

In Wyoming, child custody disputes are a sensitive and emotionally charged issue that requires the expertise of a seasoned family law attorney. The state follows the best interests of the child doctrine, which guides judges in making custody determinations that prioritize the child's well-being, health, and stability. A skilled family law attorney can help navigate the complexities of Wyoming child custody law and advocate for the client's rights.

A child custody dispute in Wyoming may be initiated through a court action, such as a divorce or separation, where the parents are unable to reach an agreement on custody and parenting time. In some cases, a third-party may also seek custody of the child, such as a grandparent or other relative.

Types of Custody in Wyoming

In Wyoming, there are two primary types of child custody: legal custody and physical custody.

  • Legal Custody: Grants the parents the right to make decisions regarding the child's education, healthcare, and overall welfare.

  • Physical Custody: Refers to the child's living arrangement and who has the right to physical custody of the child.

In addition to legal and physical custody, Wyoming law also recognizes joint custody and sole custody arrangements.

  • Joint Custody: Both parents share equal rights and responsibilities for decision-making and physical custody of the child.

  • Sole Custody: One parent has exclusive rights and responsibilities for decision-making and physical custody of the child.

Factors in Awarding Custody in Wyoming

In Wyoming, the court considers several factors when determining child custody. These include:

  • History of Domestic Violence: A history of domestic violence between the parents may be a significant factor in determining custody.

  • Parental Cooperation: The court may consider whether both parents are able to cooperate and work together in the best interests of the child.

  • Employment and Economic Stability: The court may consider a parent's employment history, education level, and financial stability when determining custody.

  • Morality and Parental Fitness: The court may consider a parent's moral character, lifestyle, and overall parental fitness when determining custody.

Modification of Custody Orders in Wyoming

In Wyoming, a court can modify a custody order if there has been a significant change in circumstances that affects the child's well-being or best interests. This may include scenarios such as:

  • Change in Employment or Income: A change in employment or income may affect a parent's ability to provide for the child.

  • Relocation: A parent's relocation to a new city or state may impact visitation and physical custody arrangements.

  • Change in Relationships: A parent's new relationships may have a significant impact on the child's welfare and custody arrangements.

A family law attorney in Wyoming can help clients navigate the complex child custody laws and advocate for their rights throughout the modification process.
