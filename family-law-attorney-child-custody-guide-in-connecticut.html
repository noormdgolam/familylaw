Family Law Attorney Child Custody Guide in Connecticut

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

In the state of Connecticut, family law attorneys play a crucial role in navigating the complex and often emotionally charged process of child custody cases. As a highly experienced family law attorney and legal consultant, we will guide you through the intricacies of child custody law in Connecticut, including state-specific statutes, common legal proceedings, expected attorney costs, and step-by-step guidance.

State-Specific Statutes and Laws

The state of Connecticut has established various laws and regulations governing child custody cases. Some of the key statutes include:

  • Conn. Gen. Stat. § 46b-56: This statute sets forth the factors that the court must consider when determining custody of a minor child.
  • Conn. Gen. Stat. § 46b-56a: This statute requires the court to consider the best interests of the child when making custody decisions.
  • Conn. Gen. Stat. § 46b-57: This statute outlines the procedures for seeking custody of a minor child.
  • Conn. Gen. Stat. § 46b-58: This statute requires the court to consider the wishes of the child when determining custody.

Types of Custody Arrangements

There are various types of custody arrangements that can be established in Connecticut, including:

  • Joint Custody: This arrangement involves both parents sharing physical and legal custody of the child.
  • Sole Custody: This arrangement involves one parent having physical and legal custody of the child, while the other parent may have visitation rights.
  • Split Custody: This arrangement involves one parent having physical and legal custody of one child, while the other parent has physical and legal custody of the other child.
  • Shared Parenting: This arrangement involves both parents sharing physical and legal custody of the child, with a focus on cooperation and joint decision-making.

Common Legal Proceedings

Child custody cases in Connecticut typically involve the following common legal proceedings:

  • Initial Custody Hearing: This hearing is held to determine temporary custody of the child.
  • Custody Trial: This trial is held to determine permanent custody of the child.
  • Discovery: This process involves the exchange of information and evidence between the parties.
  • Mediation: This process involves a neutral third-party mediator facilitating communication and negotiation between the parties.
  • Guardian Ad Litem (GAL) Appointments: A GAL may be appointed to represent the interests of the child in the case.

Initial Custody Hearing

The initial custody hearing is a critical proceeding in child custody cases. During this hearing, the court will typically consider the following factors:

  • The child's physical, emotional, and psychological well-being.
  • The parents' ability to provide a stable and loving home environment.
  • The parents' ability to cooperate and communicate with each other.
  • The child's wishes and preferences.

Custody Trial

The custody trial is a more extensive proceeding that may involve testimony from witnesses, experts, and the parties themselves. During this trial, the court will typically consider the following factors:

  • The child's best interests.
  • The parents' ability to provide a stable and loving home environment.
  • The parents' ability to cooperate and communicate with each other.
  • The child's wishes and preferences.

Step-by-Step Guidance

Here are the steps to follow when navigating a child custody case in Connecticut:

  1. Contact a family law attorney to discuss your case and determine the best course of action.
  2. File a petition for custody with the court.
  3. Attend the initial custody hearing and present evidence and testimony.
  4. Participate in discovery and mediation to gather information and negotiate a settlement.
  5. Attend the custody trial and present evidence and testimony.
  6. Receive the court's decision and appeal if necessary.
