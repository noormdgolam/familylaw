Family Law Attorney Child Custody Guide in Delaware

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Divorced or separated parents often face a complex and emotionally charged situation when determining child custody in Delaware. As a highly experienced Family Law attorney, I will provide a comprehensive guide to understanding the laws and procedures surrounding child custody in the First State.

Delaware Child Custody Laws

Delaware law prioritizes the best interests of the child when determining custody arrangements. The Delaware Code Title 13, Chapter 15 governs child custody proceedings. Key statutes include:

  • 13 Del. C. § 501(a)(1): The court shall order a custody arrangement that serves the best interests of the child.
  • 13 Del. C. § 501(a)(2): The court shall consider the wishes of the child, as expressed by the child, if the child is of sufficient age and capacity to express a preference.
  • 13 Del. C. § 501(a)(3): The court shall consider the wishes of the parents and any other relevant factors.
  • 13 Del. C. § 501(b): The court may order joint or sole custody, with the parent having the most significant relationship with the child receiving primary residential custody.

Types of Custody Arrangements in Delaware

Delaware courts recognize the following types of custody arrangements:

  • Sole Custody: One parent has sole physical and legal custody of the child.
  • Joint Custody: Both parents share physical and legal custody of the child, often with one parent having primary residential custody.
  • Shared Parenting: Both parents share equal physical and legal custody of the child, with no primary residential parent.
  • Split Custody: One child resides with each parent, often in situations where there are multiple children with different custody arrangements.

Factors Considered in Delaware Child Custody Proceedings

Delaware courts consider the following factors when determining child custody:

  • The wishes of the child, as expressed by the child, if the child is of sufficient age and capacity to express a preference.
  • The wishes of the parents.
  • The child's relationship with each parent and other individuals, including siblings and other relatives.
  • The child's needs, including physical, emotional, and educational needs.
  • The stability and continuity of the child's environment.
  • The parents' ability to cooperate and communicate effectively.
  • The parents' ability to provide a stable home environment.
  • The child's age and level of maturity.
  • The parents' employment and financial stability.
  • The presence of any domestic violence or abuse in the household.
  • The parents' mental health and substance abuse history.

Common Legal Proceedings in Delaware Child Custody Cases

The following are common legal proceedings in Delaware child custody cases:

  • Initial Custody Hearing: The court holds an initial hearing to determine temporary custody and visitation arrangements.
  • Custody Evaluation: A custody evaluator assesses the parents and child to provide an objective recommendation for custody arrangements.
  • Mediation: Parents may participate in mediation to reach a mutually agreeable custody arrangement.
  • Custody Trial: The court holds a trial to determine permanent custody arrangements.

Step-by-Step Guidance for Delaware Child Custody Proceedings

Here's a step-by-step guide to navigating Delaware child custody proceedings:

  1. Filing a Complaint: The parent seeking custody files a complaint with the court, outlining the desired custody arrangement.
  2. Serving the Other Parent: The parent seeking custody serves the other parent with the complaint and summons.
  3. Temporary Custody Hearing: The court holds an initial hearing to determine temporary custody and visitation arrangements.
  4. Custody Evaluation: A custody evaluator assesses the parents and child to provide an objective recommendation for custody arrangements.
  5. Motions and Hearings: Parents may file motions and participate in hearings to resolve disputes and address concerns.
  6. Custody Trial: The court holds a trial to determine permanent custody arrangements.
  7. Custody Order: The court issues a custody order outlining the permanent custody arrangement.

FAQs About Delaware Child Custody Law

Here are some frequently asked questions about Delaware child custody law:

  • Q: How long does a Delaware child custody case typically take? A: The duration of a child custody case varies depending on the complexity of the case and the court's schedule.
  • Q: Can I represent myself in a Delaware child custody case? A: Yes, but it is highly recommended to hire a Family Law attorney to ensure the best possible outcome.
  • Q: What is the standard for determining the best interests of the child in Delaware? A: The court considers the factors outlined in 13 Del. C. § 501(a) to determine the best interests of the child.
  • Q: Can I relocate with my child if I have custody? A: Yes, but you must provide the other parent with notice and comply with any applicable court orders.
  • Q: What if I disagree with the custody evaluation? A: You may file objections to the custody evaluation and present your own evidence in court.
