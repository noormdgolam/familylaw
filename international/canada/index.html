Overview of Canada Family Law

Canada operates under a federal Divorce Act, but property division and common-law relationships are governed by provincial and territorial laws. It's crucial to understand both jurisdictions.

Key Considerations

  • Jurisdiction: Ensure the courts in Canada have the legal authority to hear your case.
  • Asset Tracing: International divorces may complicate the tracing of overseas assets.
  • Child Relocation: Specific laws govern the movement of children out of Canada without the consent of both parents.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a US court enforce custody in Canada?

Enforcement depends on international treaties such as the Hague Convention. It is critical to consult an expert in international family law to ensure your rights are protected across borders.
