Australian family law explained. Learn about the Family Law Act 1975, the principle of no-fault divorce, and the role of the Family Court.

Family law varies drastically across borders. In Australia, navigating the legal intricacies of separation, divorce, and child custody requires specific regional knowledge.

Overview of Australia Family Law

Australia has a long-standing 'no-fault' divorce system. The primary focus in child custody disputes is the 'best interests of the child', encouraging shared parental responsibility.

Key Considerations

  • Jurisdiction: Ensure the courts in Australia have the legal authority to hear your case.
  • Asset Tracing: International divorces may complicate the tracing of overseas assets.
  • Child Relocation: Specific laws govern the movement of children out of Australia without the consent of both parents.
